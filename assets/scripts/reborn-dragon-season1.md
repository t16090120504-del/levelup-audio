## Series Overview

In a world where dragons were driven to extinction five centuries ago by the Demon King, an overworked Japanese salaryman dies in a train accident and is reborn as the last living dragon — heir to the Ancient Dragon King's bloodline. With the power to command all dragon-kind and the ability to take human form, he disguises himself as a noble and enrolls in the Royal Academy. There he crosses paths with Princess Seraphina, a fierce warrior who carries the weight of a dying kingdom on her shoulders. Their bond will reshape the fate of a world on the brink of demon revival.

My name was Ryu Tanaka. Thirty-two years old. Salaryman. Section Chief of the Third Marketing Division at Hikari Industries, where I had worked every single day for the past eight years without a single vacation.

I died on a Tuesday.

The 7:14 AM Shinkansen from Shin-Osaka to Tokyo. I remember the morning clearly because it was the first morning in three years I'd actually slept more than four hours. My alarm didn't go off. I missed the 6:30 train. For the first time in my career, I was going to be late.

Nothing.

My new body.

## Episode 2: "Human Skin"

Below the mountains stretched an endless expanse of green — forests, rivers, scattered villages with smoke curling from thatched roofs. A fantasy world. The kind I used to read about in light novels during my lunch breaks.

[beat]

[beat]

So that was fun.

## Episode 5: "Fire Beneath the Skin"
**Duration target: ~4 minutes (~600 words)**

[beat]

Her mask slipped. Just for a second. Vulnerability cracked through the warrior's composure like sunlight through ice.

"Goodnight, Lord Ashveil."

## Episode 7: "Ash and Truth"
**Duration target: ~4 minutes (~600 words)**

## Episode 9: "Scales and Kisses"
**Duration target: ~4 minutes (~600 words)**

[beat]

Seraphina was beside me before I could blink. Her hands pressed fabric against the wound, her face inches from mine, her breath warm on my cheek.

"You idiot," she hissed. "You never know your limits. You just keep pushing until something breaks."

"Occupational hazard," I managed. "In my old life they called it dedication."

She stopped. Her hands stilled. "Your old life. You never talk about it. The one before."

I met her gaze. "It wasn't interesting. Just work. Every day, every hour, work. I died on a train and my biggest regret was leaving emails unanswered."

"That's the saddest thing I've ever heard."

[beat]

"You died once already," she whispered. "I won't let it happen again."

And she kissed me.

It wasn't a dramatic, sweeping romance kiss. It was brief, firm, and over almost before I registered it. She pulled back immediately, her expression shifting to something between embarrassment and defiance, as if daring me to comment.

I didn't comment. I reached up, cupped her jaw with my hand — the one that wasn't currently bleeding — and kissed her back.

[beat]

We sat on the armory floor afterward, shoulders touching, in comfortable silence. Her head rested against mine. I could feel her heartbeat, steady and strong, and mine matched it. Or maybe her heart matched mine. With dragon senses, I could never be sure which rhythm was leading.

"This doesn't change anything," she said eventually. "I'm still the princess. You're still the last dragon. The Demon King is still coming."

"I know."

"And I'm still going to train you until you can hold your breath weapon for more than six seconds without passing out."

"I'd appreciate that, yes."

"And when the time comes — when the Demon King rises — I will fight beside you. Not behind you. Beside you."

I closed my eyes and let the warmth of her presence settle into my bones. Former salaryman. Current dragon. Apparently, also a princess's boyfriend.

My old life really was soul-crushingly boring.

## Episode 10: "The Last Dragon Rises"
**Duration target: ~5 minutes (~750 words)**

I was in Seraphina's private study — a room I now had unrestricted access to, which was either a sign of her trust or the kingdom's worst security lapse — when the ground trembled beneath us. The seal had broken.

[beat]

"Two days. Maybe less. The vanguard is fast."

High above Solaris, in dragon form, I patrolled the northern horizon. The sky was wrong — bruised purple and sickly green, the colors of demonic magic bleeding into the world. From the north, a darkness approached. Not shadows. An absence. A void that consumed light and sound and hope.

The Demon King's army.

I could feel them before I saw them — twisted creatures, once mortal, now corrupted into engines of destruction. And behind them, pulsing at the heart of the darkness like a black sun, something vast and ancient and furious.

The Demon King.

He was awake.
